About Me Today

I am currently a Bachelor of Public Administration student at the University of Southern Mindanao Main Campus. I am an irregular student because I shifted from my previous course, Bachelor of Science in Development Communication, in the same university.

Because of this shift, I now take my GEMST 03 class with second-year Computer Engineering students even though I am still in my first year. This setup requires strong adjustment because I am exposed to a different academic environment, new classmates, and higher expectations.

At first, the adjustment was difficult. I needed to keep up with lessons, understand new concepts, and adapt to different teaching styles. I also had to build confidence in interacting with new people. Despite these challenges, I remained focused and committed to my studies.

I am introverted. I stay quiet in new environments and observe first before engaging. Once I become comfortable, I show my true personality. I am friendly, approachable, and easy to get along with. I value respect and positive relationships.

I describe myself as simple, kind, and determined. I take my responsibilities seriously and complete my tasks on time. I believe that discipline and consistency are important in achieving success.

Being a shifter taught me how to adapt and remain strong. It helped me accept change and move forward with confidence. I continue to focus on my goals and improve myself every day.
